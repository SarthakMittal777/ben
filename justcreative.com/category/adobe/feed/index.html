How to Choose a Good PDF Editor?

PDF has been the go-to tool for viewing and sharing readable files for more than two decades now. Individuals and businesses alike have been using Adobe Acrobat for viewing PDF documents but you can go only so far with Adobe Acrobat unless you opt for their pricey plans.

Therefore, more and more brands are getting into the PDF services market offering PDF users amazing benefits at much more affordable fees.

If you’re in the dilemma of choosing the right PDF editor, we’ve made your work easy with our most important considerations in a good PDF editor. Read on!

Usability

The most important feature a good PDF editor should have is an intuitive interface. Users, of all experience levels, must be able to perform the most basic tasks without any hassle or without having to look around for features in the interface.

So make sure you choose the PDF editor that puts the essential features on the familiar interface.

Ability to edit and create PDFs

This goes without saying, if you need more than just PDF reading capabilities, you need to opt for a PDF editor that comes with the ability to add text, images, videos, and objects. A smart PDF editor lets you change text size alignment and flow the text as you edit it.

Other key features include watermarks, electronic signatures, stamps, headers, footers, etc. Most software these days comes packed with these features at affordable prices.

Scan and OCR

To digitize paper documents, you need to connect your PDF editor to a scanner. By doing so, you can make most of your work paperless and store all the important files in a PDF format. If your PDF editor app allows this, look no further.

Once you scan your documents, you might also want to convert the text in the scanned documents as a distinct PDF file.

Optical Character Recognition (OCR) technology identifies text from images, giving you a full-fledged text version of the scans. This text version can be converted to an editable PDF format.

Ability to collaborate

Collaboration is a must-have feature in a PDF editor, especially in the post-pandemic era where remote teams could work together on files and enable easy sharing between teams.

Your PDF editor must allow you to add multiple participants, share reviews, and annotate. Being able to open and view PDFs on the EMS Cloud Services shouldn’t be burdensome.

BYOD

BYOD stands for Bring Your Own Device, an IT policy that supports and encourages employees to use their personal devices like mobiles, tablets, and laptops to access enterprise data and systems.

You need a PDF editor that has your back when you are away from your work system. With a PDF editor app, you can view, edit, and review PDF files anywhere, anytime. So look out for a mobile app for the PDF editor you’re interested in.

Security

If you deal with confidential documents quite often, you should seek a PDF editor with password protection capabilities. This feature limits features like viewing the document, printing, filling forms, and editing. Go for a PDF editor which lets you change access permissions or even revoke access even after publishing and sharing the file.

Customer support

At some point, you might want to get in touch with the customer support and service team. Problems with products are common and a PDF editor is no different. A good PDF editor should come with proven quality customer service and care to help you get out of troubled waters.
